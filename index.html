üî¥ RED Bag - Infectious Waste

  • Contents:
    • Blood-soaked dressings and bandages
    • Used gloves, masks, gowns (contaminated)
    • Culture plates and specimens
    • Used syringes (without needles)
    • Contaminated laboratory waste
  • Treatment: Incineration or autoclaving required before disposal
  • Handling: Sealed bags, not to exceed 5kg, dispose within 24-48 hours

üü° YELLOW Bag - Pathological Waste

  • Contents:
    • Body parts and organs
    • Tissues and anatomical waste
    • Placenta and products of conception
    • Biopsy specimens
  • Treatment: Incineration ONLY (autoclave not acceptable)
  • Handling: Immediate refrigeration if disposal delayed, proper labeling

üü¢ GREEN Bag - Non-Infectious/General Waste

  • Contents:
    • Office paper and cardboard
    • Food waste from non-patient areas
    • Packaging materials (non-contaminated)
    • General clinic trash
  • Treatment: Regular municipal waste disposal
  • Handling: Can be collected by regular garbage service

üîµ BLUE Bag - Recyclable Waste

  • Contents:
    • Plastic bottles (clean, non-contaminated)
    • Paper and cardboard boxes
    • Glass bottles (non-hazardous)
    • Aluminum cans
  • Treatment: Segregate for recycling programs
  • Handling: Clean before disposal, coordinate with recycling centers

‚ö´ BLACK Bag/Container - Hazardous Waste

  • Contents:
    • Expired medications and pharmaceuticals
    • Cytotoxic/chemotherapy waste
    • Chemical reagents and disinfectants
    • Mercury-containing items (thermometers, BP apparatus)
    • Batteries and electronic waste
  • Treatment: Special disposal through DOH-accredited TSD facility
  • Handling: Separate containers, proper labeling, manifesting required

üíâ SHARPS Container (Puncture-Proof, Yellow)

  • Contents:
    • Used needles and syringes
    • Scalpel blades and surgical blades
    • Broken glass (contaminated)
    • Lancets and IV catheters
  • Treatment: Incineration through TSD facility
  • Handling: Never recap needles, fill only to 3/4 capacity, seal properly
  • Rule: Do NOT overfill - replace when 75% full

üéì Mandatory Staff Certifications Overview

All healthcare professionals must maintain valid certifications to comply with DOH and PhilHealth standards. Expired certifications can result in accreditation issues.

üíâ BLS (Basic Life Support) Certification

  • Mandatory All clinical staff (physicians, nurses, midwives)
  • Training Duration: 1 day (8 hours)
  • Validity: 2 years from date of certification
  • Content Covered:
    • CPR (adult, child, infant)
    • AED (Automated External Defibrillator) use
    • Choking relief maneuvers
    • Recognition of cardiac arrest
    • Team dynamics in resuscitation
  • Providers: American Heart Association (AHA), Philippine Heart Association, DOH-accredited centers
  • Cost: ‚Ç±3,000 - ‚Ç±5,000 per participant

üöë ACLS (Advanced Cardiac Life Support)

  • Recommended Physicians and senior nurses
  • Training Duration: 2 days (16 hours)
  • Validity: 2 years from date of certification
  • Content Covered:
    • Advanced airway management
    • ECG rhythm interpretation
    • Cardiovascular pharmacology
    • Management of cardiac arrest
    • Post-cardiac arrest care
  • Prerequisite: Valid BLS certification
  • Cost: ‚Ç±8,000 - ‚Ç±12,000 per participant

üë∂ BEmONC (Basic Emergency Obstetric & Newborn Care)

  • Mandatory If offering maternal/child health services
  • Required for: Physicians, nurses, midwives handling deliveries
  • Training Duration: 5 days (40 hours competency-based)
  • Validity: 3 years from date of certification
  • Skills Covered:
    • Normal vaginal delivery assistance
    • Postpartum hemorrhage (PPH) management
    • Pre-eclampsia/eclampsia management
    • Newborn resuscitation (NRP)
    • Manual removal of placenta
    • Assisted vaginal delivery (vacuum extraction)
    • Referral protocols for complications
  • Providers: DOH-accredited training centers only
  • Cost: ‚Ç±5,000 - ‚Ç±10,000 per participant

üíä FPCBT (Family Planning Competency-Based Training)

  • Mandatory For PhilHealth FP services reimbursement
  • Two Levels Required:
    • FPCBT 1: Natural & barrier methods, pills, injectables, IUD insertion
    • FPCBT 2: Contraceptive implant insertion/removal
  • Training Duration: 3-5 days per level
  • Validity: Permanent (but refresher courses recommended every 3 years)
  • Required for: Physicians, nurses, midwives providing FP services
  • Providers: DOH-POPCOM accredited training sites
  • Cost: ‚Ç±4,000 - ‚Ç±8,000 per level

ü¶† Infection Prevention & Control (IPC) Training

  • Mandatory All staff (clinical and non-clinical)
  • Training Frequency: Quarterly refresher sessions
  • Content Covered:
    • Hand hygiene protocols (WHO 5 moments)
    • Standard precautions and transmission-based precautions
    • Personal protective equipment (PPE) proper use
    • Safe injection practices
    • Environmental cleaning and disinfection
    • Healthcare waste segregation
    • Occupational exposure management (needlestick injuries)
  • Documentation: Training attendance sheets and post-training competency assessment

üìã PRC CPE (Continuing Professional Education)

  • Mandatory For PRC license renewal
  • Requirements per 3-year cycle:
    • Physicians: 45 CPE credit units
    • Nurses: 45 CPE credit units
    • Pharmacists: 45 CPE credit units
    • Medical Technologists: 45 CPE credit units
  • Accredited Providers: PMA, PNA, PPhA, PAMET, PRC-accredited organizations
  • Formats: Seminars, workshops, conventions, online webinars
  • Tracking: PRC online CPE portal for verification

üîê Data Privacy & Cybersecurity Training

  • Mandatory All staff with access to patient records
  • Training Frequency: Annual or upon hiring
  • Content Covered:
    • Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173) overview
    • Patient confidentiality and HIPAA principles
    • Secure handling of patient information
    • Password security and authentication
    • Phishing and social engineering awareness
    • Breach reporting protocols
  • Documentation: Signed confidentiality agreements and training certificates

üå± Occupational Safety and Health (OSH) Program

DOLE Department Order 198-18: Mandatory OSH program for all workplaces including healthcare facilities.

üíâ Needlestick Injury Prevention & Post-Exposure Protocols

  • Prevention Measures:
    • Safety-engineered devices (retractable needles, needleless systems)
    • Sharps containers within arm's reach (never overfill >75%)
    • NEVER recap needles (use one-handed scoop technique if necessary)
    • Proper disposal immediately after use
    • PPE compliance (gloves mandatory for all procedures)
  • Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP) Protocol:
    • Immediate Action: Wash wound with soap and water, irrigate mucous membranes
    • Report: Incident report within 2 hours to supervisor
    • Testing: Baseline HIV, HBV, HCV testing (employee and source patient if known)
    • HIV PEP: Start within 72 hours (preferably <2 hours), 28-day regimen
    • HBV Vaccination: If unvaccinated, administer HBV vaccine + HBIG
    • Follow-up: Repeat testing at 6 weeks, 3 months, 6 months
  • Documentation: Incident report, exposure classification, treatment record

‚öóÔ∏è Hazardous Materials Handling & Storage

  • Chemical Safety:
    • All chemicals labeled with hazard symbols and GHS classification
    • Safety Data Sheets (SDS) available for all chemicals
    • Storage in designated lockable cabinet (segregated by compatibility)
    • Spill kits readily accessible
    • Eyewash station and safety shower (if using corrosive chemicals)
  • Common Hazardous Materials:
    • Disinfectants (bleach, glutaraldehyde, formaldehyde)
    • Laboratory reagents
    • Compressed gases (oxygen cylinders)
    • Mercury-containing devices (being phased out)
  • Staff Training: Annual chemical safety orientation, SDS review

üî• Fire Safety Compliance & Evacuation

  • Fire Prevention:
    • No smoking policy strictly enforced

  • Fire Drills: Quarterly with BFP coordination, documented attendance

üîê Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173)

  • Privacy Policy Requirements:
    • Written privacy policy displayed prominently
    • Data collection purposes clearly stated
    • Patient consent before processing sensitive personal information
    • Data retention and disposal procedures documented
  • Patient Rights Under DPA:
    • Right to be informed about data collection
    • Right to access own medical records
    • Right to correct inaccurate information
    • Right to object to processing (with limitations)
    • Right to data portability
  • Data Protection Officer (DPO):
    • Designation of DPO (can be existing staff)
    • Registration with National Privacy Commission (NPC)
    • DPO responsibilities: Compliance monitoring, breach response
  • Data Breach Notification:
    • Report to NPC within 72 hours of discovery
    • Notify affected individuals if high risk to rights
    • Document breach investigation and corrective actions
  • Penalties: ‚Ç±500,000 - ‚Ç±5,000,000 fine, 1-6 years imprisonment

üö´ Anti-Sexual Harassment Act (RA 7877)

  • Clinic Policy Required:
    • Written anti-sexual harassment policy
    • Posted in conspicuous locations
    • Included in employee handbook
    • Annual orientation for all staff
  • Committee on Decorum and Investigation (CODI):
    • Designation of committee members (minimum 3)
    • At least one woman member
    • Complaint receiving and investigation procedures
    • Confidentiality protocols
  • Complaint Process:
    • Written complaint submission
    • CODI investigation (30 days maximum)
    • Due process for accused and complainant
    • Sanctions for substantiated cases

üë®‚Äçüë©‚Äçüëß‚Äçüë¶ Expanded Anti-Trafficking in Persons Act (RA 10364)

  • Clinic Responsibilities:
    • Mandatory reporting of suspected trafficking victims

    • Provision of medical services to victims (free if indigent)

üíº Labor Code of the Philippines (PD 442)

  • Employee Rights and Benefits:
    • 13th month pay (December or before)
    • Service Incentive Leave (5 days minimum per year)
    • SSS, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG contributions
    • Overtime pay (125% of hourly rate, 130% on rest days/holidays)
    • Night shift differential (10% for 10pm-6am work)
  • Required Postings:
    • Working hours and meal break schedule
    • Minimum wage rates (per NWPC region)
    • Employee rights and benefits poster
    • Anti-sexual harassment policy
    • Occupational safety and health standards
  • Employment Contracts:
    • Written contract specifying duties, salary, benefits
    • Probationary period: Maximum 6 months
    • Regular employment status after probation
    • Just cause or authorized cause for termination

üè• Medical Device Regulation (RA 10918)

  • FDA Registration Requirements:
    • All medical devices must have FDA Certificate of Product Registration (CPR)
    • Class A (low risk): Self-certification
    • Class B (moderate risk): FDA review required
    • Class C & D (high risk): Clinical evidence required
  • Clinic Responsibilities:
    • Purchase devices only from FDA-licensed establishments
    • Verify CPR before use
    • Report adverse events to FDA
    • Proper maintenance and calibration
  • Common Devices Requiring Registration:
    • Blood pressure monitors, glucometers
    • Diagnostic imaging equipment (ultrasound, X-ray)
    • Surgical instruments and kits
    • Laboratory analyzers

üíä Pharmacy Law (RA 10918) & FDA Regulations

  • If Clinic Has Pharmacy/Dispensing Area:
    • Licensed pharmacist required during operating hours
    • FDA License to Operate (LTO) for pharmacy
    • Proper storage (temperature monitoring, security)
    • Controlled substances logbook (if dispensing)
    • Adverse drug reaction (ADR) reporting to FDA
  • Generic Medicines Priority:
    • Generic name must be prescribed (RA 6675)
    • Generic substitution allowed unless physician specifies "No Substitution"
    • Patient education on generic equivalence

üîç Professional Malpractice & Liability

  • Professional Liability Insurance:
    • Highly recommended for all physicians
    • Coverage: ‚Ç±1,000,000 - ‚Ç±5,000,000 per occurrence
    • Protects against malpractice claims
  • Risk Management Strategies:
    • Informed consent for all procedures
    • Thorough documentation (SOAP format)
    • Clear communication with patients
    • Adherence to clinical practice guidelines
    • Timely referrals when indicated
    • Incident reporting and root cause analysis
  • If Facing Malpractice Claim:
    • Do NOT discuss case with patient or family without lawyer
    • Notify insurance company immediately
    • Preserve all medical records (do not alter)
    • Seek legal counsel (preferably medical malpractice specialist)
    • Report to PRC if administrative complaint filed
